On just another normal Monday, the world changed. The universe had reached a threshold humanity didn’t even know existed, and it was time to finally be integrated into the vast multiverse. A world where power is the only thing that one can truly rely on.

Jake, a seemingly average office worker, finds himself thrust into this new world. Into a tutorial filled with dangers and opportunities. In a world that should breed fear and concern, an environment that makes his fellow coworkers falter, Jake instead finds himself thriving.

Perhaps… Jake was born for this kind of world, to begin with.

Great Start, but I can feel the downhill slope

Reviewed at: Chapter 657 - Nevermore: First Floor - START OF BOOK 10
by Dr1nsurance Sunday, July 14, 2024 4:20:10 AM

The series had a pretty great start, and despite all the advantages Jake found himself with, it still felt like a captivating underdog story with a smart MC using every trick in their arsenal to barely come out on top. Now over 600 chapters in and it's just boring how easily everything comes around to Jake. I can't remember the last time there was any real sense of possibility of failure or danger. The last *really dangerous* enemy fought was the Termite King, and despite Jake's inner monologue on how powerful it was and how it had a fair chance of actually killing him, I never felt that feeling at all. It was just a moment to highlight how big of fish in a very small pond Jake has become. And honestly I could look past that just fine, OP MCs can be just as fun as underdog MCs. But one thing I can't get past though is the tonal shift I've noticed as the series has gone on. The first arcs, while still having plenty of welcomed moments of comedy to break the tension, were dark. They established clearly and quickly that the new reality was brutal. Every action was important, every split-second the difference between life and death, and even just looking at the wrong person at the wrong time could get your entire civilization annihilated before you knew what was up. If you wanted to survive, you needed to be smart and play to your strengths. Now, many of those same situations are treated as jokes. I've lost count of how many "My dad is way stronger than your dad"-esque scenarios Jake has found himself involved in. For someone who claims to fully want to stand on his own two feet and not rely on the reputation of the Malefic Viper, he sure does rely on it a lot. I'm not even annoyed that Jake is taking full advantage of Villy, and actually think it would be more annoying if he didn't, but his connection to Villy takes away the stakes of pretty much every faction interaction outside Earth. It pretty much handwaves away whatever conflicts Jake's arrogance would undoubtedly guarantee with basically everyone he meets. This DO NOT ENGAGE policy towards Jake just doesn't feel earned, not just yet. And lastly, my biggest pet peave, is the harem trope dynamics introduced several hundred chapters in. Talk about annoying. So many characters' motivations on how to find their Path lost to make room for how they now want, and sometimes succeed, to get into Jake's pants. I'm starting to dread new female character introductions. I don't know if this one will immediately get into a cringe-worthy pick-me dance with the other girls, or if she will receive some semblance of character development and then join the pick-me dance. Only time will tell I suppose. As disparaging as my review is, I really do want to continue liking this series, because damn does it nail what it does right. I don't know any other series where I've been as invested in the worldbuilding as I am in this one. Everytime something new gets introduced I love the exposition dump that accompanies it. It reminds you just how large the multiverse is and how little we've seen it. I also love when mysteries that have been in the background for entire arcs finally get their moment to shine. The author hasn't yet disappointed with delivering a satisfying payout for these moments. I've really enjoyed this series so far, but I really don't know for how much longer that'll continue. The other reviews don't exactly seem promising

This is one of my favorite stories already. It's a lot like Randidly Ghosthound, but with a better pace. It isn't quite as long, yet, but there aren't big bursts of chapters followed by long spurts of silence. It is a story following a ranger (obviously), his colleagues (sometimes too much), and some gods (funny, powerful fellows). Some might say Jake is OP, but he is reasonably OP, everything is explained in ways that primarily make sense with story-logic. As said previously, it does have more than just Jake as the POV occasionally which can detract somewhat, but not much. If you like those then you'll love this, or if you don't know them, after you catch up in this one, go check those out. They're quite a bit longer (as of 2/17/21), but why not love several great stories simultaneously? It's fun.

A Pleasure to Read!

Reviewed at: Chapter 652 - Onwards, To Nevermore
by Dusky van Doom Saturday, October 14, 2023 10:16:55 AM

Primal Hunter is the one of the best, if not *the best*, novels you'll find out there. I'm reading it on Patreon since ages and didn't regret spending my money even once.

The chapters are long (3,5k words) and consequently updated Monday till Friday. The author interacts with the community in the comments quite often. 

The story and writing are awesome. It's a bloody but not really dark world, were everyone strives to become stronger. The friendship between the Chosen and his Patron is, what's making this story so enjoyable to follow for me. Good humor mixed in, without feeling stupid and with time more and more characters are joining the MCs journey. Each of them having their own story to tell, their own fears to worry about.

At the beginning, like with most system based novels, there is a lot of writing about numbers going up or down and contemplating over item-stats. But with the continuing story, this becomes less the focus and understanding skills becomes more a thing than just some numbers.

The arcs are great and clearly cut from each other, with time for the MC and he's friends to have some time in between to reflect on what they have experienced and learned. 

My conclusion: definitely give this one a try!
